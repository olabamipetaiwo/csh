The previous engineer left csh in a half-working state.
Conduit's containerised build environments use csh to
chain diagnostic commands together, and we cannot ship
without it.

Here is what we know:

  1. The lexer (lex.l) is complete and working.

  2. The parser compiles but drops all arguments after the program name. Any command with arguments breaks silently.

        echo "ls -l /tmp" | ./csh
        # only `ls` runs -- -l and /tmp are dropped

  3. Redirect-in (<) is completely unimplemented in the parser. The token is ignored.

        echo "grep stdio.h < /tmp/inc.txt" | ./csh
        # < is silently ignored

  4. The interpreter handles a single command with redirect-out only. It does not handle pipes at all.

        echo "ls /usr/include | grep stdio" | ./csh
        # does nothing
